# Contact Research Strategies

This reference provides techniques for finding decision maker names, email addresses, phone numbers, and LinkedIn profiles.

## Decision Maker Identification

### Strategy 1: Website Research

**Where to Look**:
1. **About Us / Our Team page** - Most common location
2. **Leadership / Management page** - For larger organizations
3. **Contact Us page** - Sometimes lists key personnel
4. **Footer** - May include founder/owner name
5. **Blog author bios** - Often written by leadership
6. **Press releases** - Quotes from executives

**What to Look For**:
- Owner, Founder, CEO, President
- Managing Partner, Managing Director
- Practice Manager (healthcare, legal)
- Principal (professional services)
- General Manager (hospitality, retail)

**Example Search Pattern**:
```
site:example.com ("owner" OR "founder" OR "CEO" OR "president")
```

---

### Strategy 2: LinkedIn Company Page

**Process**:
1. Search for company on LinkedIn
2. Go to company page
3. Click "See all [X] employees"
4. Filter by:
   - Title: Owner, CEO, President, Founder, Managing Partner
   - Seniority: Owner, Partner, C-level

**Title Patterns by Industry**:
- **Healthcare**: Dr. [Name], Owner; Practice Manager; Medical Director
- **Law Firms**: Managing Partner; Senior Partner; Founding Partner
- **Real Estate**: Broker/Owner; Principal Broker; Managing Broker
- **Restaurants**: Owner; General Manager; Operating Partner
- **Professional Services**: Managing Partner; Principal; President

---

### Strategy 3: Google Search Patterns

**Effective Search Queries**:
```
"[Business Name]" owner
"[Business Name]" founder
"[Business Name]" CEO
"[Business Name]" president
"[Business Name]" "founded by"
"[Business Name]" "owned by"
```

**Look in**:
- News articles
- Press releases
- Local business directories
- Chamber of Commerce listings
- Industry association directories
- Award announcements

---

### Strategy 4: Business Registration Databases

**Sources**:
- Secretary of State business entity search (by state)
- Better Business Bureau (BBB)
- Dun & Bradstreet
- Local business licenses
- Professional licensing boards (for regulated industries)

**What You'll Find**:
- Registered agent name (often the owner)
- Officers and directors
- Principal place of business
- Registration date

## Email Address Discovery

### Strategy 1: Website Email Patterns

**Common Patterns**:
- firstname@company.com
- firstnamelastname@company.com
- first.last@company.com
- flast@company.com
- firstname.l@company.com
- info@company.com (general, not decision maker)

**Where to Find**:
- Contact page
- Team member pages
- Email signatures in blog posts
- Footer
- Privacy policy / legal pages

---

### Strategy 2: Email Pattern Inference

**Process**:
1. Find ANY email from the company (even general ones)
2. Identify the pattern
3. Apply pattern to decision maker name

**Example**:
- Found: john.smith@example.com (from team page)
- Pattern: firstname.lastname@domain
- Decision maker: Jane Doe
- Inferred: jane.doe@example.com

**Validation**:
- Use email verification tools (Hunter.io, NeverBounce)
- Check for bounce-back if sending test email
- Look for LinkedIn "Contact Info" (sometimes shows email)

---

### Strategy 3: LinkedIn Contact Information

**Process**:
1. Find decision maker on LinkedIn
2. Click "Contact Info" (if available)
3. May show email address, especially if:
   - You're a 1st degree connection
   - They've made it public
   - You have LinkedIn Premium/Sales Navigator

**Alternative**:
- Send LinkedIn message requesting email
- Connect first, then access contact info
- Check their website link in profile (may lead to contact page)

---

### Strategy 4: Email Finding Tools

**Tools**:
- **Hunter.io**: Domain search + email verification
- **RocketReach**: Contact database
- **Clearbit**: Company and contact data
- **Lusha**: LinkedIn extension for contact info
- **ZoomInfo**: B2B contact database (premium)

**Best Practice**:
- Use multiple tools for verification
- Validate emails before adding to database
- Mark confidence level (verified vs. inferred)

---

### Strategy 5: Google Search for Email

**Search Patterns**:
```
"[Name]" "[Company]" email
"[Name]" "@[domain]"
site:linkedin.com "[Name]" "[Company]" email
```

**Look in**:
- Conference speaker lists
- Webinar registration pages
- Industry directories
- Press release contact information
- Professional association listings

## Phone Number Discovery

### Strategy 1: Website Contact Information

**Where to Look**:
- Contact Us page (primary)
- Header/footer (often main line)
- Location pages (for multi-location businesses)
- Team member pages (sometimes direct lines)
- Appointment booking pages

**What You'll Find**:
- Main business line (most common)
- Direct lines (less common)
- Department-specific numbers

---

### Strategy 2: Google Business Profile

**Process**:
1. Search "[Business Name] [City]" on Google
2. Check the business profile card on the right
3. Phone number is prominently displayed
4. Often the most accurate and up-to-date

**Bonus**:
- Also shows address, hours, website
- Customer reviews
- Photos
- Q&A section

---

### Strategy 3: Business Directories

**Sources**:
- Yelp
- Yellow Pages
- Better Business Bureau
- Chamber of Commerce
- Industry-specific directories
- Local business associations

**Validation**:
- Cross-reference across multiple sources
- Most recent listing is usually most accurate
- Call to verify if uncertain

---

### Strategy 4: LinkedIn Profile

**Process**:
1. Find decision maker on LinkedIn
2. Click "Contact Info"
3. May show phone number if:
   - You're connected
   - They've made it public
   - You have Premium/Sales Navigator

**Alternative**:
- Check company page for main number
- Look for phone in profile headline or summary
- Check website link in profile

---

## LinkedIn Profile Discovery

### Strategy 1: LinkedIn Search

**Basic Search**:
1. Go to LinkedIn search
2. Enter: "[Name] [Company Name]"
3. Filter by "People"
4. Look for matching profile

**Advanced Search** (Sales Navigator):
- Filter by company name
- Filter by title (Owner, CEO, etc.)
- Filter by location
- Filter by seniority level

---

### Strategy 2: Google Search for LinkedIn

**Search Pattern**:
```
site:linkedin.com "[Name]" "[Company Name]"
site:linkedin.com/in/ "[Name]" "[Company Name]"
```

**Why This Works**:
- Google indexes LinkedIn profiles
- Often faster than LinkedIn's own search
- Can find profiles even if not logged into LinkedIn

---

### Strategy 3: Company Page Employee List

**Process**:
1. Find company page on LinkedIn
2. Click "See all [X] employees"
3. Search/filter for decision maker
4. Sort by "Most relevant" or filter by title

**Best For**:
- Larger companies with many employees
- When you know the company but not the exact name
- Discovering multiple decision makers

---

### Strategy 4: Mutual Connections

**Process**:
1. Search for the person
2. Check for mutual connections
3. Ask for introduction if appropriate
4. View "People Also Viewed" for similar profiles

**Networking Approach**:
- Warm introduction is always better
- Mention mutual connection in outreach
- Higher response rates with referrals

## Data Quality Standards

### Email Quality Levels

| Level | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Verified** | Confirmed via email verification tool or direct communication | Use with confidence |
| **Inferred** | Pattern-based, not verified | Mark as "inferred", verify before large campaigns |
| **General** | info@, contact@, etc. | Use only if no personal email found |
| **Not Found** | No email discovered | Mark as "Not publicly available" |

### Phone Quality Levels

| Level | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Direct** | Direct line to decision maker | Highest priority for calls |
| **Main** | Main business line | Will need to ask for decision maker |
| **Unverified** | Found but not confirmed | Verify before adding to database |
| **Not Found** | No phone discovered | Mark as "Not found" |

### LinkedIn Quality Levels

| Level | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Confirmed** | Profile matches name, company, title | Use for outreach |
| **Likely** | Profile matches but missing some details | Review before outreach |
| **Not Found** | No matching profile | Mark as "Not found" |

## Research Workflow

### Standard Process (15-20 minutes per lead)

1. **Find Business Website** (2 min)
   - Google search: "[Business Name] [City]"
   - Verify it's the correct business

2. **Identify Decision Maker** (5 min)
   - Check website About/Team page
   - Search LinkedIn company page
   - Google: "[Business Name] owner"

3. **Find Email** (3 min)
   - Check website contact page
   - Infer pattern from other emails
   - Use Hunter.io or similar tool

4. **Find Phone** (2 min)
   - Check Google Business Profile
   - Check website contact page
   - Verify in business directories

5. **Find LinkedIn** (3 min)
   - LinkedIn search: "[Name] [Company]"
   - Google: site:linkedin.com "[Name] [Company]"
   - Check company employee list

6. **Verify & Document** (5 min)
   - Cross-reference information
   - Note confidence levels
   - Add to database

## Batch Research Optimization

### For 50+ Leads

**Phase 1: Business Discovery** (Parallel)
- Use map tool to research all businesses simultaneously
- Each subtask finds: name, website, address, phone

**Phase 2: Decision Maker Identification** (Parallel)
- Use map tool with business websites
- Each subtask finds: decision maker name, title

**Phase 3: Contact Enrichment** (Parallel)
- Use map tool with decision maker names
- Each subtask finds: email, LinkedIn profile

**Phase 4: Validation** (Sequential)
- Run validation script
- Identify gaps
- Manual research for high-value leads with missing data
