# Opportunity Evaluation Framework

This framework provides a structured process for evaluating the viability of potential business opportunities. The goal is to produce a data-driven confidence score that informs the decision to invest in a new venture.

## 1. Initial Screening

- **Market Size**: Is the total addressable market (TAM) large enough to support a profitable business?
- **Profitability**: Is there a clear path to profitability? What are the potential margins?
- **Competition**: Who are the main competitors? What are their strengths and weaknesses?
- **Unfair Advantage**: Does the proposed venture have a unique advantage (e.g., proprietary technology, exclusive partnerships, unique business model)?

## 2. Deep Dive Analysis

- **Customer Analysis**: Who is the target customer? What are their needs and pain points?
- **SWOT Analysis**: A detailed analysis of the venture's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
- **Financial Projections**: Detailed financial models including revenue forecasts, cost analysis, and cash flow projections.

## 3. Confidence Score Calculation

A confidence score is calculated based on the following weighted factors:

| Factor | Weight |
| :--- | :--- |
| Market Size & Growth | 20% |
| Profitability & Margins | 30% |
| Competitive Landscape | 20% |
| Unfair Advantage | 20% |
| Execution Plan Feasibility | 10% |

Each factor is scored on a scale of 1-10, and the weighted average is calculated to produce a final confidence score. A score above 95% is required to proceed with the venture.
