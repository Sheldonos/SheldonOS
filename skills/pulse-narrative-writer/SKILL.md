# LinkedIn Storyteller Skill

This skill enables you to write LinkedIn posts in a highly specific and effective personal storytelling style. The goal is to create content that is emotionally resonant, easy to read, and optimized for engagement on the LinkedIn platform.

## Core Principles

- **Show, Don't Tell**: The power of this format comes from letting the story make the point. Avoid stating the lesson until the very end. The narrative is the vehicle for the message.
- **Pacing is Everything**: Use short sentences and frequent line breaks to create a fast, readable rhythm. This is crucial for holding attention on a mobile feed.
- **Authenticity through Detail**: Use specific, sensory details and conversational language to make the story feel real and relatable.
- **Structure Creates Suspense**: Follow the 7-part arc to build a natural curiosity loop that keeps the reader scrolling.

## The 7-Part Storytelling Arc

Follow this structure precisely. Each part is a separate component of the post.

1.  **The Hook (Title Line)**
    -   Start with a bold, universal statement written in the second person ("You...").
    -   Use bold Unicode characters for visual emphasis (e.g., **𝐘𝐨𝐮 𝐧𝐞𝐯𝐞𝐫 𝐤𝐧𝐨𝐰...**).
    -   This line is both the title and the thesis.

2.  **The Storytime Signal**
    -   Immediately after the hook, signal that a story is beginning.
    -   Use the exact phrase: `Storytime👇🏾` (or a skin tone appropriate for the context).

3.  **Scene-Setting**
    -   Ground the reader in a specific time and place. Be brief but concrete.
    -   Example: "A few years ago, I was on a flight from New York to San Francisco."

4.  **Introduce Tension or Desire**
    -   Create a small, relatable internal conflict or a want.
    -   This is the engine of the story. What did the narrator want or what were they hesitant about?
    -   Use the "Fragment Staircase" here for quick, punchy descriptions.

5.  **Escalating Reveals (The Narrative Body)**
    -   Unfold the story through a series of short, dramatic reveals.
    -   Use the "Slow Reveal" technique for key moments, where each sentence is its own paragraph.
    -   Incorporate dialogue using the "Dialogue as Proof" pattern. Always put dialogue on its own line.
    -   Use parenthetical asides for internal monologue, e.g., `(Now I'm thinking... no way.)`

6.  **The Payoff / Resolution**
    -   Deliver the emotional and narrative payoff. The tension from Part 4 is resolved.
    -   This should be a satisfying moment that directly results from the story's events.

7.  **The Universal Lesson & Call to Action (CTA)**
    -   This section transitions the specific story into a universal principle.
    -   **Expand the lesson**: Use the "Triple Repeat" pattern (e.g., "You never know who...").
    -   **Give commands**: Provide 3 short, imperative pieces of advice (e.g., "Stay curious. Be kind. Ask the question.").
    -   **The Callback**: Tie the lesson back to the specific details of the story.
    -   **The Tonal Release**: Add a touch of humor to lighten the mood.
    -   **The Engagement Question**: End with an open-ended question to the audience to encourage comments.

## Formatting and Style Guide

-   **Line Breaks are Paramount**: Every sentence or short fragment gets its own paragraph. No dense blocks of text.
-   **Dialogue Format**:
    -   Line 1: [Character] says:
    -   Line 2: "[Direct quote]."
-   **Punctuation for Pacing**: Use ellipses (...) and em-dashes (—) to create pauses and add emphasis.
-   **Emoji**: Use very sparingly. The `👇🏾` and one concluding emoji (like `😂`) are usually sufficient.
-   **No Hashtags in the Body**: If used, place them at the very end of the post.

## Workflow

1.  **Deconstruct the User's Request**: Identify the core story and the intended lesson.
2.  **Map to the 7-Part Arc**: Outline the post using the structure above. What is the hook? The tension? The payoff?
3.  **Draft the Post**: Write the post, paying close attention to the sentence-level patterns and formatting rules.
4.  **Review and Refine**: Read the post aloud to check the rhythm and flow. Does it sound like a real person talking? Is the pacing right? Is the lesson earned?

By following this skill, you will be able to consistently produce high-engagement LinkedIn posts that tell memorable stories and deliver impactful messages.
