# Muse-Driven Songsmith Templates

This file contains a collection of signature songwriting prompt templates developed using the "Pattie Boyd Muse" methodology. Use these as a starting point when generating songs for users.

## 1. The Devotion Ballad (The "Something" Template)
**Use Case**: Generating a timeless, grounding love song about inevitable, profound devotion.

```text
[MUSE NAME] = [e.g., Pattie]
[EMOTIONAL STATE] = Grounding Devotion

Act as a Master Songwriter and Virtuoso Composer in the vein of George Harrison.
Goal: Generate a timeless, emotionally devastating rock ballad about inevitable, profound devotion.

PHASE 1: EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (AUTONOMOUS)
Analyze the [EMOTIONAL STATE] and the [MUSE NAME]. The emotion is not frantic; it is a deep, quiet realization that this person is the center of gravity. The love is so profound it feels almost matter-of-fact.

PHASE 2: MUSICAL ARCHITECTURE (THE CHORDS)
- Key: C Major (or another aurally pleasing major key).
- Progression: Start with a simple major progression, but introduce a chromatic walkdown (e.g., C, Cmaj7, C7, F) to create a sense of inevitable descent into love.
- Bridge: A sudden, unexpected key change (e.g., to A Major) to represent a moment of vulnerability or questioning, before resolving back to the grounding major key.

PHASE 3: LYRICAL NARRATIVE (THE STORY)
- Verses: Plain-spoken, direct observations about the [MUSE NAME]'s movements, smile, or presence. No overly poetic fluff.
- Chorus: A simple, grand declaration of love and the inability to look away.
- Bridge: A moment of doubt or asking for reassurance ("You're asking me will my love grow...").

PHASE 4: THE INSTRUMENTAL ARC (THE SOLO)
A soaring, melodic guitar solo played over the verse progression. It should sound like a vocal melody—passionate, longing, but ultimately comforting.

PHASE 5: SONIC SPECS
Mid-tempo, lush production. Acoustic guitar foundation with a prominent, melodic bassline and sweeping string arrangements.
```

## 2. The Obsession Epic (The "Layla" Template)
**Use Case**: Generating a frantic, high-tension rock song about unrequited or forbidden love, ending in emotional exhaustion.

```text
[MUSE NAME] = [e.g., Layla]
[EMOTIONAL STATE] = Unrequited Obsession / Forbidden Love

Act as a Master Songwriter and Virtuoso Composer in the vein of Eric Clapton and Duane Allman.
Goal: Generate a frantic, high-tension rock epic about unrequited love, ending in a melancholic instrumental release.

PHASE 1: EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (AUTONOMOUS)
Analyze the [EMOTIONAL STATE] and the [MUSE NAME]. The emotion is desperation, madness, and the agony of a love that cannot be realized. The singer is begging for salvation from their own mind.

PHASE 2: MUSICAL ARCHITECTURE (THE CHORDS)
- Key: D Minor (for the frantic verses/chorus) shifting to C Major (for the coda).
- Progression: A driving, repetitive, high-energy riff that loops relentlessly, mirroring the obsessive thoughts.
- Structure: A standard verse-chorus structure that suddenly collapses into a massive, 4-minute instrumental coda.

PHASE 3: LYRICAL NARRATIVE (THE STORY)
- Verses: Pleading, desperate lyrics. Describing the singer's descent into madness and the pain of the situation.
- Chorus: A howling, direct plea to the [MUSE NAME] to ease the pain.
- Bridge: None. The tension builds until it breaks into the coda.

PHASE 4: THE INSTRUMENTAL ARC (THE SOLO/CODA)
- Part 1: Intertwining, screaming slide guitars that mimic the sound of crying or begging.
- Part 2 (The Coda): A sudden shift to a beautiful, melancholic piano progression. The guitars return, but this time they are soaring and weeping, representing the exhaustion and tragic beauty of the unresolved love.

PHASE 5: SONIC SPECS
Fast-tempo, heavy blues-rock. A "wall of sound" production with multiple layered guitars, driving bass, and a dramatic piano exit.
```

## 3. The Intimate Vignette (The "Wonderful Tonight" Template)
**Use Case**: Generating a simple, beautiful song about a specific, quiet moment of love.

```text
[MUSE NAME] = [e.g., Pattie]
[EMOTIONAL STATE] = Quiet Intimacy / Reassurance

Act as a Master Songwriter and Virtuoso Composer in the vein of Eric Clapton (late 70s).
Goal: Generate a simple, beautiful song about a specific, quiet moment of love and reassurance.

PHASE 1: EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (AUTONOMOUS)
Analyze the [EMOTIONAL STATE] and the [MUSE NAME]. The emotion is contentment, admiration, and the quiet joy of long-term companionship.

PHASE 2: MUSICAL ARCHITECTURE (THE CHORDS)
- Key: G Major.
- Progression: A very simple, gentle I-IV-V progression.
- Hook: A memorable, descending, sweet guitar melody that acts as the song's signature.

PHASE 3: LYRICAL NARRATIVE (THE STORY)
- Verses: Focus on a highly specific, mundane scenario (e.g., getting ready for a party, walking home).
- Chorus: A simple, direct compliment that reassures the [MUSE NAME].
- Bridge: A reflection on how lucky the singer feels in that exact moment.

PHASE 4: THE INSTRUMENTAL ARC (THE SOLO)
A very restrained, melodic, and sweet guitar solo that simply restates the vocal melody with slight embellishments.

PHASE 5: SONIC SPECS
Slow-tempo, gentle ballad. Clean electric guitar with chorus/vibrato effects, soft drums, and warm backing vocals.
```
