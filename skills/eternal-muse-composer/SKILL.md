# Muse-Driven Songsmith

This skill provides a framework for writing professional, high-end, emotionally devastating songs based on the "Pattie Boyd Muse" methodology, structured using the Amir Mushich prompt-writing framework. It reverse-engineers the songwriting DNA of George Harrison and Eric Clapton to create songs of historical caliber.

## Core Philosophy

The "Pattie Boyd Muse" approach to songwriting is built on the following principles:
1. **The Muse as Reflection**: The muse is not just an object of affection, but a mirror reflecting the songwriter's deepest vulnerabilities, insecurities, and desires.
2. **Emotional Extremes**: The songs must operate at the extremes of human emotion—either the purest, most grounding devotion (e.g., "Something") or the most agonizing, destructive obsession (e.g., "Layla").
3. **Musical Tension and Release**: The emotional narrative must be mirrored in the chord structure. Use chromatic walkdowns, unexpected key changes, and tension-building riffs to convey longing and resolution.
4. **Specificity in Simplicity**: The lyrics should be plain-spoken but hold grand sentiments. Avoid overly poetic fluff; speak directly to the core of the feeling.
5. **The Coda/Bridge Shift**: Every great muse song has a moment of musical departure—a bridge that shifts the perspective or a coda that provides emotional release after intense tension.

## The "Muse-Driven" Prompt Framework

When generating a song or songwriting prompt, use this highly structured anatomy, adapted from the Amir Mushich methodology:

### 1. Variable Declaration
Declare variables at the top for easy swapping (e.g., `[MUSE NAME] = Layla`, `[EMOTIONAL STATE] = Unrequited Obsession`).

### 2. Role Assignment
Always assign a specific professional role to the AI (e.g., `Act as a Master Songwriter and Virtuoso Composer in the vein of George Harrison and Eric Clapton.`).

### 3. Goal Statement
Provide one clear sentence stating the output goal (e.g., `Goal: Generate a timeless, emotionally devastating rock ballad about forbidden love.`).

### 4. Phase-Based Instructions
Break the songwriting process down into distinct phases:
- **PHASE 1: EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (AUTONOMOUS)** - Analyze the muse, the relationship dynamic, and the core emotional conflict.
- **PHASE 2: MUSICAL ARCHITECTURE (THE CHORDS)** - Define the key, the progression, and the tension points (e.g., chromatic walkdowns, key changes).
- **PHASE 3: LYRICAL NARRATIVE (THE STORY)** - Write the verses, chorus, and bridge using plain-spoken but profound language.
- **PHASE 4: THE INSTRUMENTAL ARC (THE SOLO/CODA)** - Describe the emotional function of the instrumental sections (e.g., a soaring slide guitar solo, a melancholic piano coda).
- **PHASE 5: SONIC SPECS** - Define the genre, tempo, instrumentation, and production style.

## Key Techniques to Apply

When writing songs using this skill, ensure you incorporate these techniques:

- **The "Something" Technique (Devotion)**: Use a major key with chromatic walkdowns to create a sense of inevitable, grounding love. Keep lyrics simple and direct ("Something in the way she moves...").
- **The "Layla" Technique (Obsession)**: Use a driving, repetitive riff to convey restlessness. Build tension in the verses and release it in a howling chorus. Include a drastic musical shift (like a piano coda) to represent the aftermath of the emotional explosion.
- **The "Wonderful Tonight" Technique (Intimacy)**: Focus on a specific, mundane moment (e.g., getting ready for a party) and elevate it to a profound declaration of love. Use a simple, descending guitar hook.
- **The "For You Blue" Technique (Joyful Subversion)**: Take a standard blues progression and inject it with overwhelming, happy-go-lucky joy.

## Workflow

1. **Understand the Request**: Identify the desired emotional state (devotion, obsession, heartbreak, joy) and the genre.
2. **Select a Template**: Choose the most appropriate template from the `references/templates.md` file.
3. **Customize the Prompt**: Fill in the variables and adapt the role, phases, and sonic specs to fit the specific request.
4. **Deliver the Output**: Present the finalized song lyrics, chord progression, and structural analysis to the user.
