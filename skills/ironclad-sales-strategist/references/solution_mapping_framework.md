# Solution Mapping Framework

This framework provides a structured way to connect a customer's critical business challenges directly to your solutions. The goal is to move the conversation away from product features and toward business outcomes.

## The Mapping Matrix

Use this matrix to create a clear, one-to-one mapping between the customer's pain and your value.

| Critical Business Challenge (The "What") | Root Cause (The "Why") | Business Impact ($) | Our Solution/Capability | How It Solves the Problem |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Example:** "60-70% of IT budget is consumed by legacy system maintenance." | Monolithic, 29-year-old SAP systems require extensive manual support and specialized, expensive talent. | $200M - $300M annually in excess maintenance costs. | **IBM Consulting Application Modernization** + **Cloud Pak for Applications** | We migrate legacy workloads to a modern, containerized platform, reducing maintenance overhead by 30-40% through automation and standardized architecture. |
| **Example:** "Risk of production-stopping cyber breaches in OT environments." | Convergence of IT and OT systems has created new, unmonitored attack surfaces. Lack of unified threat visibility. | $5M+ per incident in potential downtime, plus reputational and safety risks. | **IBM Security QRadar SIEM/SOAR** | Provides a single pane of glass for threat detection across both IT and OT networks, enabling rapid response to threats before they impact production. |
| **Example:** "250+ AI projects with no unified governance creates compliance and safety risks." | Each division is using different tools and platforms (heavy Azure OpenAI dependency) with no central oversight for model risk, bias, or lifecycle management. | Potential EU AI Act penalties up to 6% of global revenue ($1.9B+). Risk of safety incidents from unexplainable AI. | **IBM watsonx.governance** | Establishes a centralized AI governance framework for any model, on any cloud. It provides model lifecycle management, bias detection, and explainability to ensure compliance and safety. |

## Instructions for Use

1.  **Start with the Pain:** Always begin with the customer's stated business challenge, taken from their own reports or statements.
2.  **Identify the Root Cause:** Why does this problem exist? What is the underlying technical or process failure?
3.  **Quantify the Impact:** Use the "Cost of Doing Nothing" models to assign a dollar value to the problem.
4.  **Introduce Your Solution:** Name the specific product, service, or capability that addresses the root cause.
5.  **Explain the "How":** In simple terms, describe the mechanism by which your solution solves the problem and delivers the desired business outcome.
