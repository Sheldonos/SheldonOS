# Stakeholder Communication Plan

Effective communication tailored to each stakeholder is critical for navigating a complex enterprise sale. This plan outlines the key personas, their primary concerns, and the messaging that resonates most effectively with each.

## The Four Key Personas

In any large deal, you will encounter four primary archetypes. You must identify and engage with all of them.

| Persona | Role & Focus | Key Questions They Ask | Our Message to Them |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Economic Buyer** | The person with the budget (CFO, LOB Head). Focused on financial outcomes and strategic alignment. | "What is the ROI?"<br>"How does this help us meet our corporate goals?"<br>"What is the total cost of ownership?" | **Focus on Value Realization.**<br>"This is a ${{Total Value}}M value proposition with a {{Payback Period}}-month payback period that directly supports your [Strategic Initiative]." |
| **Technical Buyer** | The person who validates the technical feasibility (CIO, CTO, Chief Architect). Focused on architecture, security, and integration. | "Does this integrate with our existing stack?"<br>"Is it secure and compliant?"<br>"How difficult is it to implement and maintain?" | **Focus on Integration & Risk Reduction.**<br>"Our solution is built on an open, hybrid-cloud architecture that integrates seamlessly with your existing investments in [Their Tool 1] and [Their Tool 2]. It meets the highest standards for security and compliance." |
| **User Buyer** | The people who will actually use the solution (Developers, Plant Managers, Business Analysts). Focused on usability and impact on their daily work. | "Will this make my job easier or harder?"<br>"Is it easy to learn?"<br>"How does this help me do my job better?" | **Focus on Productivity & Ease of Use.**<br>"This tool will automate the repetitive parts of your job, allowing you to focus on more valuable work. We've seen user productivity increase by 50% or more." |
| **Champion** | The person who is passionate about your solution and will advocate for it internally. Often a mid-level manager who feels the pain most acutely. | "How can I convince my boss this is the right solution?"<br>"What data do you have to help me build the internal business case?" | **Focus on Arming Them with Data.**<br>"Here is the complete business case, a presentation you can share with your leadership, and a case study from a similar company. Let's work together to make this happen." |

## Communication Strategy

1.  **Identify Your Champion First:** Find the person who is most passionate about solving the problem. They will be your guide to the organization.
2.  **Arm Your Champion:** Give them the tools they need to sell internally. Your job is to make them look good.
3.  **Validate with the Technical Buyer:** Ensure the solution is technically sound and get their buy-in on the architecture.
4.  **Get Support from the User Buyer:** Show them how the solution will make their lives better. A solution that users hate will fail.
5.  **Present the Business Case to the Economic Buyer:** Once you have support from the other three personas, present the financial case to the person who holds the purse strings. The conversation should be a formality at this point.
