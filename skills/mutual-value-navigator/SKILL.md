# Customers Win, Suppliers Win Strategist

This skill provides a framework for developing and executing strategic account management plans that create win-win outcomes for both your company and your key customers. It is based on the principles outlined in the book "Customers Win, Suppliers Win" by Noel Capon and Gus Maikish.

## Core Principle: The Win-Win Philosophy

The fundamental principle of this skill is that sustainable success in key account management is achieved when both the customer and the supplier win. This requires a shift from a transactional mindset to a strategic partnership approach, where the focus is on creating mutual value and achieving shared objectives.

## Application Workflow

Follow these steps to apply the Customers Win, Suppliers Win methodology:

### Step 1: Assess and Classify Your Key Accounts

1.  **Identify Potential Key Accounts**: Determine which customers have the potential to be strategic partners.
2.  **Apply the Four Strategic Zones Framework**: Classify each key account into one of the four strategic zones by assessing:
    *   **Your company's ability to impact the customer.**
    *   **The customer's ability to impact your company.**
3.  **Consult the Reference Guide**: For a detailed explanation of the Four Strategic Zones Framework, read `/home/ubuntu/skills/customers-win-suppliers-win-strategist/references/frameworks.md`.

### Step 2: Develop the Strategic Account Plan using the Congruence Model

For each strategic account, develop a comprehensive plan using the four-part Congruence Model:

1.  **Select and Conceptualize the Portfolio**: Define the scope and objectives for the account.
2.  **Organize and Manage the Account**: Structure your team and processes to effectively serve the customer.
3.  **Build the Team**: Recruit, train, and reward your account management team.
4.  **Formulate and Execute Strategy**: Develop and implement an account-specific strategy with clear coordination and control mechanisms.

*   For a detailed breakdown of the Congruence Model, refer to the `frameworks.md` reference file.

### Step 3: Focus on Creating Win-Win Outcomes

Ensure your strategic account plan is designed to achieve a win-win outcome:

*   **For the Customer to Win**: Your plan should help the customer succeed in their market, gain a competitive advantage, and achieve their strategic objectives.
*   **For Your Company to Win**: Your plan should lead to superior financial returns, increased wallet share, and a sustainable long-term relationship.

### Step 4: Implement and Continuously Improve

*   Execute the strategic account plan, ensuring alignment across all functions.
*   Continuously monitor performance and make adjustments as needed.
*   Foster a culture of partnership and mutual trust.
*   Regularly review the account's classification within the Four Strategic Zones and adapt your strategy accordingly.
